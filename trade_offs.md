# Trade Offs
 
This note explores the benefits of balance in life. In this context, balance is not a symptom of mediocrity, but a skill that is learned and practiced for tuning your efforts toward your values and goals.
 
## Kindness to Self
 
Despite appearances, the argument for promoting kindness to self is more straightforward than the argument against it. Also, this argument for promotion is not based on the "seek happiness, not success" line of thinking.
 
### Why You Should Be Kinder to Yourself?
 
This line of argument is very personal to the author, but it will likely resonate with others dealing with idealism and self-loathing. The starting point is the intuitive, general idea that **doing hard things (DHT) is good**. Arguments for this premise are outside the scope of this section, so we'll only work with its related implications. The next step is to connect this idea to the issue of **having basic ambitions (HBA)**. Before getting to the connection between DHT and HBA, let's review some instances of HBA below. These are mostly ordinary activities, and there's nothing wrong with them on their own. They become a problem once someone adopts them as goals or ambitions and wastes considerable time and resources on them. Each instance is paired with a mainstream tool used for over-indulgence, and a simple antidote.
 
| Basic Need | Description | Mainstream Tools | Antidote |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Food** | As a necessity for every living organism, everyone puts in some effort to obtain food. The issue is the famous notion of "living to eat" instead of "eating to live." | Food delivery apps like Uber Eats, which give you fast, easy access to the most palatable items without any cooking effort or planning. You get what you crave almost immediately. | A combination of fasting and meal prep. |
| **Entertainment** | Unlike food, this one isn't necessary at all. No one has ever actually died of boredom. | Infinite feeds you can doom-scroll on (e.g. YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels, TikTok). | Still an open question — deactivating social media and switching to a black-and-white phone screen helps, though I haven't found a solid antidote for myself yet. |
| **Lust** | The essence of life. The goal here is not getting into the nuances of intimacy or the evolutionary biology. If the need is not clear to you, think of the urge you feel before browsing Pornhub (if you've never visited it, close this document and keep enjoying your life). | Multi-billion dollar industries mainstream access to this need by promoting hook-up culture, and pseudo-access to it through porn. | Striving for a meaningful relationship with a romantic partner. |
 
DHT and HBA have a two-fold relationship:
 
1. Overcoming HBA through antidotes is itself a DHT.
2. Moving beyond HBAs leads to more sophisticated goals, and pursuing them requires a lot of DHTs.
In summary, you need to DHT to avoid HBA.
 
On the other hand, avoiding HBAs increases your capacity for DHT. That's where kindness to self enters the picture. DHT requires mental energy. The journey is meaningful, and meaning acts like fuel for your engine, but the path is still full of hiccups. If you keep beating yourself up for falling short or slipping up, you'll waste that mental energy. Moreover, idealism sucks the fuel out of your engine by taking the meaning out of the tasks in each step of the journey.
 
In conclusion, on the path of pursuing your values, be kind to yourself so you can sustain doing the right things. Doing the easy right things is trivial, so you need to be capable of doing the hard things. Doing the hard things keeps you out of the valley of basic ambitions.
